EPAM CSR: educational projects for kids, trainers and teachers, IT 60+

We not only strive to develop the best solutions for our customers we passionately act for good. Our employees are empowered to lead socially responsible educational, environmental and community initiatives for the good of the world and everyone in it.

Let's get acquainted with some of EPAMs educational social projects.

EPAM EKids

This is our volunteer program of online and offline classes, where young girls and boys explore the world of software engineering and learn the basics of programming, robotics, animation, game development, STEM, English, Logic and Math. We teach young children the basics of programming with Scratch; for more senior kids, we offer Python, Java Script, Java, CSS/HTML, and more.

eKids was founded in 2015. To date, more than 1,300 EPAMers have become volunteers and helped more than 4,500 children and youth in 19 countries to get closer to IT. The program is attended by children of EPAM employees, as well as kids from SOS Children's Village, refugees from Syria, Iran and Ukraine, kids from the volunteer association Rano (which helps families with premature babies), and kids from difficult family backgrounds.

Today, eKids continues to actively develop and expand its borders. Every enrollment campaign sees more and more children who want to learn programming, and so the program needs trainers. We welcome all who are passionate about teaching and who demonstrate ongoing commitment and dedication to students and learning. We will help you with all aspects of teaching classes and provide support and continuous mentoring from experienced eKids trainers along the way.

EPAM EKids Garage

A tech lab with workstations, robotics constructions, 3D printers and a wide range of other tools, the Garage is well-suited for studying, brainstorming, experimenting and inventing. EPAM Garage strengthens the advanced programming skills of exceptional students from EPAM eKids. The learning process is based on the concepts of STEM education, which underlines the crucial role of science, technology, engineering and mathematics.

Program coordinators say that one important feature is the dialogue between engineering practitioners and schoolteachers. To further this end, they are writing educational materials for a computer science course that will unite the expertise of engineers and the pedagogical knowledge of teachers.

Today, EPAM Garage has 11 trainers and 52 students based in Minsk.

Entry-Level Programming With Scratch

This is a free training course for anyone who wants to teach children the basics of programming and computer science with Scratch. The course was created by experienced EPAM eKids and eKids Garage volunteers to help beginning instructors.

Entry-Level Programming with Scratch teaches inexperienced trainers how to teach kids the basics of programming and computer studies and to involve them in coding and creative projects. During the course, you will:

  • learn the features of Scratch programming language;

  • create an individual course adapted for a certain group of kids;

  • take part in new creative projects together with kids and other trainers;

  • develop teamwork skills while being a part of an educational project.

The course comprises twelve lessons that can be logically divided into blocks: animation, interactive stories, and personal project preparation. Each lesson includes a detailed study plan, theory and examples from real projects, options for exercises and assignments, tips for explaining the material covered, techniques for explaining concepts, and more.

IT 60+

It's never too late to learn! This was our motto when we launched the program in 2019. IT 60+ is an educational program for seniors: parents or grandparents of EPAM employees or their relatives, as well as people from our communities.

The goal of the initiative is to share our knowledge in computer science with seniors in our community. We believe that this program will allow seniors to be confident and to keep their professional skills up to date and hopefully help in job hunting, even for retirees.

The program includes basic knowledge on computer usage and studies in MS Excel, Word, presentations, e-banking, mobile applications, workshops on internet security, and more.

More than 75 participants have successfully passed the course.

The Inclusive Coding Program

This socially important initiative, founded in 2018, encourages children and young people with disabilities to code and to design, develop and construct robots. It also promotes inclusion at EPAM and provides equal opportunities for all people.

Using Lego Mindstorms, the program provides a fun environment for kids to learn basics of coding and use their creativity. During Java, Python and Scratch classes, teenagers get acquainted with programming and the basic syntax of Java and run their first programs. Interactive classroom activities help kids to develop effective teamwork and collaboration skills.
